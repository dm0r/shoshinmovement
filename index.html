The Beginning

In 2014, something unique came to Cairns. Shoshin Movement Studio opened its doors as North Queensland's first and only movement-focused gym. Founded by David D'Amore, a former tradesman turned movement specialist who had experienced his own journey from chronic pain to physical freedom, Shoshin Movement wasn't just another fitness center—it was a philosophy made physical.

初心

The name "Shoshin" (初心) comes from a Zen Buddhist concept meaning "beginner's mind"—an attitude of openness, eagerness, and lack of preconceptions when learning, even at an advanced level. This wasn't about perfection. It was about embracing the journey, celebrating small victories, and finding joy in the process of becoming stronger, more mobile, and more capable.

For twelve years, we helped hundreds of people in Cairns improve their quality of life through better movement. We watched complete beginners learn to handstand. We saw people in their 60s move better than they did in their 30s. We built a community that believed movement isn't just exercise—it's the foundation of a vibrant, pain-free life.

The Journey

2014

The Birth of Shoshin Movement

David D'Amore brought his holistic approach to Cairns—combining strength, flexibility, handstand, and playful movement in a way the region had never seen. The response was immediate. From day one, people were drawn to this different way of training.

The Tiny Studio Era

Our first home was a cozy 75 square meter space. Picture this: up to 25 people crammed into that tiny studio, training together, laughing together, sweating together. It was tight, it was hot, but it created an incredible vibe. This is where the Shoshin community was born.

Spreading Our Wings

As our community grew, so did our need for space. We moved to a larger facility at 82 Grafton Street in the heart of Cairns CBD, inside the Mainstreet Arcade. A breath of fresh air. Room to move. Room to grow. This became the home where Shoshin truly flourished.

Building the Team

The best students became teachers. Will Stormont, Holly Muir, Rossi Postler, and Annina Strengell joined the coaching team, each bringing their unique gifts to our tribe. The gym evolved from one person's vision into a collective of passionate movement practitioners, all united by the philosophy of the beginner's mind.

2014-2026

Twelve Years of Movement

From handstands to mobility flows, from first-timers to advanced practitioners, from age 13 to 70+—Shoshin Movement became a second home for hundreds of people seeking something more than a conventional gym experience. We taught people to move without pain, build real functional strength, find joy in physical challenge, and embrace the beginner's mind at every level.

What Made Us Different

The Beginner's Mind

Shoshin (初心) — the beginner's mind. When you're learning to handstand, you'll fall. A lot. Your first pull-up attempt might not get you off the ground. Your hamstrings might scream in your first mobility class. That's exactly where you're supposed to be.

We taught our members that difficulty isn't failure—it's the entry point to growth. The beginner's mind means approaching every challenge with openness, curiosity, and zero ego. Whether you were 15 or 65, whether you'd never exercised before or you were an advanced athlete, everyone started somewhere.

Movement as Medicine

We believed that movement shouldn't just be about aesthetics or performance. It should improve your quality of life. That meant:

  • Moving without pain
  • Playing with your kids without getting hurt
  • Maintaining independence as you age
  • Building a body that's strong, supple, and resilient

A Holistic Approach

Our method built the foundations of strength, flexibility, and skill through a progressive and holistic approach—suitable for all levels and abilities. We combined:

  • Gymnastics and calisthenics for bodyweight strength
  • Mobility and flexibility work for joint health
  • Handbalance for focus and body awareness
  • Playful movement because we never forgot how to have fun

Thank You

After twelve remarkable years, Shoshin Movement Studio will close its doors in February 2026.

To every member who walked through our doors—whether you stayed for a week or a decade—thank you. You trusted us with your bodies, your goals, and your time. You showed up when it was hard. You celebrated victories, big and small. You embraced the beginner's mind and proved that anyone, at any age, can move better.

To Will, Holly, Rossi, Annina, and every coach who shared their knowledge and passion—thank you for building this community alongside me. You made Shoshin Movement more than just my vision; you made it a home for hundreds of people.

To Cairns—thank you for embracing something different. For being willing to try handstands, explore mobility, and redefine what "fitness" could mean. Shoshin Movement was Cairns' first and only movement-focused gym, and while that chapter is closing, I hope the impact continues in how you move through life.

Movement has always been about more than physical ability. It's about curiosity, resilience, and the willingness to try. It's about showing up with a beginner's mind, again and again. That's what Shoshin Movement stood for, and that philosophy doesn't end when the doors close.

Keep moving. Keep learning. Keep approaching life with openness and curiosity.

David D'Amore
Founder & Program Director
